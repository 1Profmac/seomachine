- AI usage among 50+ adults nearly doubled: 18% (2024) → 30% (2025) — AARP
- 47% of adults in their 50s know about and use AI/chatbots; only 25% of those 70+ — AARP

- 55% of people 50+ have used some type of AI (voice or text) — U of Michigan
- Gen Z 83% use AI, Millennials 73%, Gen X 60%, Boomers 52% — workplace surveys

### The Training Gap
- Only 11% of workers 55+ have received AI training vs. 30% of workers 18-44
- 70% of workers over 45 expect AI to transform their roles within 3 years
- Nearly half of workers 45+ have received ZERO employer-sponsored AI training
- 47% of workers 50+ WANT AI training in their line of work
- AI literacy requirements in job postings grew 70% in the past year

### Confidence/Attitudes
- 54% of 50+ adults feel confident they can learn and adapt to new tech
- 81% want to learn more about AI RISKS
- 58% want to learn more about AI BENEFITS
- 68% concerned AI may reduce human interactions
- Baby Boomers: 35% drop in tech confidence; Gen X: 25% drop
- 40% of workers 45+ fear partial/full job automation

- Skills gaps risk $5.5 trillion in global market losses
- Workers 55-64 stay median 10.1 years vs. 3.0 years for ages 25-34
- Upskilling costs 6X less than hiring externally
