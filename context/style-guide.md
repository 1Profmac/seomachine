# Learn More Technologies — Style Guide

This guide defines writing conventions, formatting standards, and editorial guidelines for all LMT content.

---

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Capitalization

**Headlines & Subheadings**: Title Case (Capitalize Major Words)

**Brand Names — Always Write Exactly**:
- Learn More Technologies (full company name)
- LMT (abbreviation, after first use)
- 50+TechBridge (community brand — capital T, capital B, no spaces)
- SecureStep.ai (B2B platform)
- Pioneers (always capitalized when referring to enrolled members)
- B-Roll (always hyphenated, both capitalized)

**Industry Terms**:
- AI (always caps)
- WIOA (always caps — Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act)
- ETPL (always caps — Eligible Training Provider List)
- MBE (always caps — Minority Business Enterprise)
- AgeTech (camelCase, capital A capital T)

### Numbers

**When to Spell Out**: One through nine
**Use Numerals**: 10 and above

**Exceptions — ALWAYS use numerals for**:
- Percentages: 74%, 3X
- Money: $850 billion, $5,000, $98/participant
- Stats: 347 Pioneers, 23 organizations
- Age references: adults 50+, workers over 55

**Large Numbers**:
- $850 billion (spell out billion)
- $76 trillion (spell out trillion)
- $5,000 (use comma)
- 347 (no comma needed)
- 50,000 (Pioneer goal — use comma)

### Punctuation

**Oxford Comma**: Yes (A, B, and C)

**Em Dashes**: — (em dash, no spaces)
- Usage: For emphasis or parenthetical statements
- "The technology gap — when you actually look at the data — is an investment gap."

**Ellipses**: Avoid. Be direct instead.

### Abbreviations & Acronyms

**First Use**: Spell out, then acronym in parentheses
- "Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)"
- "Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL)"
- Exception: AI, MBE — universally known in context

**Latin Abbreviations**: Avoid. Use plain English.
- "for example" not "e.g."
- "that is" not "i.e."

## Word Choice & Usage

### Mandatory Terminology

**Say This** → **Not That**:
- "adults 50+" → "seniors" / "elderly" / "senior citizens" / "the aging population"
- "Pioneers" → "students" / "participants" / "users" / "trainees"
- "experienced workers" → "older workers" (unless citing research)
- "adults 50+" → "aging workforce" / "older demographic"
- "investment gap" → "skills gap" (when discussing why 50+ aren't trained)
- "your experience is your advantage" → "it's easy" / "just simply"
- Name actual tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Zoom) → "use a digital tool" / "AI platforms"
- "completion rate" → "graduation rate" (we're not a school)
- "3X industry completion rate" → "high completion rate" (always be specific)

### Words to Avoid
- "senior" / "elderly" / "aging population" (patronizing)
- "helping seniors" / "helping the elderly" (savior complex)
- "just" / "simply" / "easy" (minimizing)
- "very" / "really" / "actually" (filler)
- "click here" / "read more" (non-descriptive links)
- "we believe" (vague — show data instead)
- "thought leader" (show, don't claim)

### Inclusive Language
- "adults 50+" is the standard — always
- Never gendered unless quoting someone specific
- "they/their" for generic references
- Respect agency — these are capable professionals, not a problem to solve

## Formatting Standards

### Text Formatting

**Bold**: Key stats, proof points, important takeaways
- "**347 Pioneers** have completed our program at **3X the industry completion rate**."

**Italics**: Emphasis (sparingly), titles of publications

**ALL CAPS**: Never in body text. Only for standard acronyms (WIOA, ETPL, MBE, AI)

### Lists

**Bulleted Lists**:
- Capitalize first word
- Period if complete sentence, no period if fragment
- Parallel structure (all sentences or all fragments)

**Numbered Lists**:
- Sequential steps, rankings, ordered priorities
- Same capitalization/punctuation rules as bullets

### Callout Boxes / Key Takeaways

Format as blockquote:
```markdown
> **Key Takeaways**
> - 347 adults 50+ have completed our program at 3X the industry rate
> - AI usage among adults 50+ doubled in one year (18% → 30%)
> - Only 11% of workers 55+ have received any AI training
```

## Content Structure

### Article Introduction (150-250 words)
1. **Direct answer** (1-2 sentences): Answer the query immediately — for AI search
2. **Hook** (1-2 sentences): Shocking stat or bold statement
3. **Problem** (2-3 sentences): What it costs, who's failing
4. **Promise** (1-2 sentences): What reader will learn
5. **Credibility** (1 sentence): 347 Pioneers, 3X rate, MBE-certified

**Primary keyword in first 100 words. Always.**

### Section Length
- **Minimum**: 150 words per section
- **Maximum**: 500 words (break into subsections if longer)
- **Ideal**: 250-350 words per main section

### Conclusion (150-250 words)
1. **Recap**: 3-5 key takeaways
2. **The question**: Reframe — "The question isn't whether you can afford to invest. It's how much more you're willing to lose."
3. **CTA**: learnmoretechnologies.com/workforce/ (B2B) or /join-now/ (individual) + Calendly
4. **Signature**: Brian McKinney, Founder, Learn More Technologies | MBE Certified | Former AARP Community Development Manager | Austin, TX

### BANNED Stats — NEVER Use
- ~~1,200+~~ (old Pioneer count)
- ~~85%~~ (old stat)
- ~~45 sites~~ (old stat)
- ~~62%~~ (old stat)
- ~~500~~ (old Pioneer goal — now 50,000)

### Citing Sources
- Always cite statistics with sources
- Link to original source when possible
- Include year of data
- Preferred sources: AARP, BLS, Census Bureau, RAND, DOL, Urban Institute, HBR

### Presenting Numbers
- Use % symbol: 74% (not "percent")
- Use $ for money: $850 billion
- Include commas: 50,000 not 50000
- Round large numbers: "$850 billion" not "$850,000,000,000"

## Editing Checklist

Before publishing any content:

**Stats & Brand**:
- [ ] All stats are current approved numbers (347, 3X, 74%, 23)
- [ ] No banned stats present
- [ ] "adults 50+" used (never seniors/elderly)
- [ ] "Pioneers" used (never students/participants)
- [ ] Brand names spelled correctly (50+TechBridge, MBE, WIOA)

**Structure**:
- [ ] Direct answer in first 1-2 sentences
- [ ] Key Takeaways block after introduction
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] 5-8 H2 sections with keyword variations
- [ ] FAQ section with 4-6 questions
- [ ] CTA to /workforce/ and/or /join-now/
- [ ] Author signature block

**Quality**:
- [ ] Every claim backed by data or credential
- [ ] Active voice predominantly
- [ ] No filler words (very, really, just, simply)
- [ ] 8th-10th grade reading level
- [ ] Sounds like Brian — direct, data-first, mission-driven
