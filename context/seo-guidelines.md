# SEO Guidelines for Learn More Technologies Content

This document outlines SEO best practices and requirements for all LMT blog and article content to maximize organic search visibility and rankings.

## Content Length Requirements

### Target Word Counts
- **Standard Blog Post / LinkedIn Article**: 2,000-3,000 words (target: 2,500)
- **Pillar Content / Comprehensive Guides**: 3,000-5,000 words maximum
- **LinkedIn Short Posts**: 300-600 words
- **Landing Page Copy**: 800-1,500 words

### Important Length Guidelines
- **Maximum for most articles**: 3,000 words
- **Maximum for pillar content**: 5,000 words (e.g., the $850B flagship article at 3,054 words is ideal)
- If a topic requires more, break it into a series (like the Overlooked Workforce 8-post series)
- Quality over quantity — don't pad. Every paragraph should contain data, insight, or action.

## Keyword Optimization

### Keyword Density Guidelines
- **Primary Keyword**: 1-2% density
- **Secondary Keywords**: 0.5-1% density each
- **LSI Keywords**: Sprinkle throughout naturally
- Natural integration is critical — never force keywords

### Critical Keyword Placement
Primary keyword MUST appear in:
- [ ] H1 headline (preferably near the beginning)
- [ ] First 100 words of article
- [ ] At least 2-3 H2 subheadings
- [ ] Last paragraph / conclusion
- [ ] Meta title (within first 60 characters)
- [ ] Meta description
- [ ] URL slug

### Keyword Integration — LMT Style
- **Natural language first**: Write for decision-makers, optimize for search engines
- **Use variations**: Don't repeat exact phrase robotically
  - "AI training for adults 50+" → "training experienced workers in AI" → "50+ workforce AI skills"
- **Question formats**: Include conversational variations
  - "Is it too late to learn AI at 50?" → "Can older workers learn AI?"
- **Semantic keywords**: Use related terms to support topical authority
  - For "workforce training 50+": include "WIOA," "digital skills," "upskilling," "experienced workers," "age discrimination"

## Content Structure Requirements

### Heading Hierarchy

#### H1 (Title)
- **Only one H1 per article**
- Include primary keyword naturally
- 60 characters or less for SERP display
- Compelling and benefit-focused
- Should answer: "What will the buyer/reader gain from this?"

#### H2 (Main Sections)
- **5-8 H2 sections** for standard articles
- At least **2-3 should include keyword variations**
- Descriptive and keyword-rich
- Can be standalone — readers should understand the argument flow from H2s alone

#### H3 (Subsections)
- Nested under H2s (never skip from H2 to H4)
- Break complex sections into digestible chunks
- Include keywords where natural

### Article Structure Template

```markdown
# [H1: Compelling Title with Primary Keyword]

[Direct answer to the query in first 1-2 sentences — for AI search optimization]

## Introduction (150-250 words)
- Hook: Shocking stat or bold statement
- Problem: What's broken and what it costs
- Promise: What the reader will learn/gain
- Credibility: 347 Pioneers, 3X rate, MBE-certified (in first 100 words)

> **Key Takeaways**
> - [Core finding #1 with specific number]
> - [Core finding #2]
> - [Core finding #3]
> - [Core finding #4 if needed]

## [H2: Main Section 1 — Include Keyword Variation]
### [H3: Subsection if needed]
- Data/statistics with citations
- Real examples from LMT experience
- No fluff

## [H2: Main Section 2]
[Continue with 5-8 total H2 sections]

## [H2: FAQ — What Decision-Makers Are Asking]
- 4-6 questions in natural prompt language
- Direct answer in first sentence, then expand
- Target People Also Ask + AI prompt formats

## Conclusion (150-250 words)
- Recap key points (3-5 takeaways)
- Include primary keyword
- CTA: learnmoretechnologies.com/workforce/ (B2B) or /join-now/ (individual)
- Calendly link for consults

---

**Author**: Brian McKinney, Founder, Learn More Technologies
**Credentials**: MBE Certified | Former AARP Community Development Manager
**Last Updated**: [Date]
```

## Meta Elements

### Meta Title
- **Length**: 50-60 characters
- **Format**: `[Primary Keyword]: [Benefit] (2026 Guide)` or `[Primary Keyword] | Learn More Technologies`
- **Examples**:
  - "AI Training for Adults 50+: Why It's Not Too Late (2026)"
  - "WIOA Digital Skills Training for Older Adults | LMT"
  - "The $850 Billion Cost of Ignoring Experienced Workers"

### Meta Description
- **Length**: 150-160 characters
- **Must include**: Primary keyword, value proposition, CTA verb
- **Formula**: `[Problem/Question]? [Solution with stat]. [CTA].`
- **Example**: "AI usage among adults 50+ doubled last year. 347 have completed our program at 3X industry rates. See what works." (134 chars)

### URL Slug
- **Format**: `/blog/[primary-keyword-phrase]`
- Lowercase, hyphens, 3-5 words, no stop words
- Examples: `/blog/ai-training-adults-50-plus`, `/blog/wioa-digital-skills-training`

- Descriptive and keyword-rich: "explore our workforce training program"
- Never "click here" or "read more"
- Place most important internal link in first 200 words when possible

## AI Search Optimization (GEO/AICO)

### Direct-Answer-First Principle
- **Answer the query directly in the first 1-2 sentences** before the narrative hook
- Put the core answer in the meta description too
- Do NOT bury the answer behind 200+ words of context

### TL;DR / Key Takeaways Block
- Every article includes a Key Takeaways block after the introduction
- 3-5 bullet points, each a complete standalone claim with specific numbers
- This is NOT a table of contents — it's the article's conclusions up front

### Authority Signaling
- **Author attribution**: "Brian McKinney" — never "Team" or brand name
- **Credentials**: MBE Certified, Former AARP Community Development Manager
- **Last updated date**: Visible on the page
- **Year in titles**: Include for time-sensitive topics

### FAQ Sections as Prompt Targets
- Write questions in natural prompt language (how real people ask)
- Answer each directly in first sentence, then expand
- 4-6 questions per article
- Source questions from: Reddit, LinkedIn comments, workforce board conversations, People Also Ask

### Embedded Media
- Embed at least one relevant YouTube video per article (prefer LMT's own: $850B series, sizzle reel)
- Cross-validation between article and video boosts AI citation likelihood

## Readability Optimization

### Target Reading Level
- **Goal**: 8th-10th grade (Flesch-Kincaid)
- Decision-makers are busy — make it scannable

### Sentence Structure
- **Average length**: 15-20 words
- **Maximum**: 25 words (break longer sentences)
- **Active voice**: 80%+ active
- **Variety**: Mix short punchy sentences with longer data-rich ones

### Paragraph Structure
- **Length**: 2-4 sentences
- **One idea per paragraph**
- **Single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis** (LMT style — "The math is not close.")
- **Subheadings every 300-400 words**

## E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)

### Experience
- Brian's firsthand experience: AARP community development, 347 Pioneers trained, 23 organizations served
- Real stories from libraries, senior centers, workforce agencies, faith communities
- Not theory — lived practice

### Expertise
- MBE-certified
- Curriculum already built and tested
- 3X industry completion rate (audit-proof)
- Quantified outcomes (74% confidence increase)

### Authoritativeness
- Cite AARP, BLS, Census, RAND, DOL, HBR, Urban Institute
- Reference WIOA legislation directly
- Include specific dollar amounts and percentages

### Trustworthiness
- Every stat has a source
- Acknowledge limitations honestly
- Never overpromise
- Use approved stats only (347, 3X, 74%, 23 orgs)
- Never use banned stats (1,200+, 62%, 85%, 500 goal)
